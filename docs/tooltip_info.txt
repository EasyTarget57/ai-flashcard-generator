My suggested guide
How to create your CSV files

You can use ChatGPT or another AI assistant to create the CSV files for you.

1. Prepare your source material

Choose any learning material:

🎬 Video subtitles or transcripts
📷 Photos of class notes
📄 PDF or Word class materials
📝 Copied text or lesson notes

For YouTube videos, download or copy the subtitles as plain text first.

2. Give the material to an AI assistant

Upload or paste your source material into ChatGPT or another AI assistant.

Then use this prompt:

[Copy prompt]

Create flashcards from the provided learning material for someone learning [LANGUAGE].

Generate two CSV files: vocabulary.csv and sentences.csv.

vocabulary.csv:
Front,Back,Pronunciation,Notes

sentences.csv:
Front,Back,Pronunciation

Guidelines:
- Front: text in the language being learned.
- Back: English translation.
- Pronunciation is optional. Use romaji for Japanese. Leave it empty if not needed.
- Notes is optional and may contain useful context such as noun, verb, grammar, or usage notes.
- Extract useful vocabulary from the source material.
- Prefer the base/dictionary form of verbs for vocabulary cards.
- Ignore filler words and unnecessary repetition.
- Remove duplicate cards.
- Preserve natural sentences from the source material when useful.
- You may also create natural example sentences that demonstrate vocabulary in different contexts.
- Prefer useful, natural flashcards over trying to reach a specific number of cards.
- Return valid CSV.
3. Paste the CSVs here

Copy the contents of vocabulary.csv and sentences.csv into the corresponding fields and click Submit.

I would remove the 15–30 limit

Definitely.

I think your new rule is much better:

Prefer useful, natural flashcards over trying to reach a specific number of cards.

The 15–30 instruction can cause an LLM to:

ignore useful vocabulary because it already reached 30
invent weak cards to reach 15
arbitrarily select cards

For a 5-minute beginner CI video, maybe there are 18 useful words. Fine.

For a 30-page class PDF, maybe there are 120. Also fine.

The user can review/delete cards on your Flashcards screen afterwards. That's actually one of the advantages of the UI you're building.
